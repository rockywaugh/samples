Summary Tab

The Summary tab in the Account View is the default landing page when accessing an Account. The Summary tab displays basic customer account information and the CPE devices associated with the account—organized by the service the device provides: video, data, or voice. A sample is shown below.

The Summary tab contains the following panels.

Panel

Displays

Neighborhood

Account location in a map view. To view the larger Node map, click the pop-out icon ().

Summary

Account street address as provided by the billing system, Amp data, and Account Status.

Recent IVR Calls

A list of calls that the customer initiated to TWC Customer Care and which were routed through the Interactive Voice Response (IVR) system. For additional details click the More... link or the IVR tab.

Video

Set-top boxes on the account including MAC Address, Serial number (if different from MAC Address), status and health. RIO separates the devices by type; including: ADSG, Scientific Atlanta (SA), Motorola, and Hydra—including any associated IP STBs.

Data

Cable modem devices on the account, including MAC Address, Serial number (if different from MAC Address), status, and health.

Voice

MTA devices on the account including Mac Address for MTA and cable modem, Serial number (if different from MAC Address), status, and health bar.

 

ADSG STBs

For Advanced DOCIS Set-top Gateway (ADSG) devices (a set-top box with a DOCSIS cable modem), RIO displays the MAC Address for both the STB and the embedded cable modem. A sample is shown below.

 

Motorola STBs

The information RIO displays for Motorola set-top boxes is slightly different from the Scientific Atlanta (SA) boxes. A sample Video panel from an Account Summary tab with Motorola data is shown below.

RIO displays overall status and health for Motorola devices.

Motorola data is not always retrievable and RIO may time out waiting for a response. In these cases, attempt to reload the page to display data.

Hydra STBs

The Hydra-class STB uses the Hosted Navigator (HNAV) application in support of the whole-house DVR product. For Hydra-class STBs, RIO displays the MAC address for the embedded set-top box (eSTB) and cable modem (CM) components along with the cable modem’s connectivity and status.
