Quarantine/Unquarantine

Quarantining a modem or MTA basically turns it into a brick and prevents it from being used. A modem may be quarantined for a variety of reasons and may be quarantined via other customer service tools, besides RIO (e.g. billing).

Although you can view whether or not a modem has been quarantined, the action to unquarantine/quarantine is available only to users with special permissions for this feature.

RIO displays quarantine information on the device Summary page. A sample is shown below.

RIO displays the quarantine reason, if applicable, on the Account Summary page, below the modem hyperlink.

Unquarantine a Modem

You must have special permissions to use the Unquarantine function.

To unquarantine a modem:

Navigate to the applicable modem.

On the device Summary tab, click Unquarantine in the Actions Menu. RIO displays the Unquarantine Account dialog box.

Click Remove next to the reason(s) you want to remove. If the modem is quarantined for multiple reasons, RIO displays each reason with a Remove button.

Click Done on the dialog box. RIO removes the quarantine for the reason(s) selected. If all reasons for quarantine are removed, RIO updates the status in the Realtime panel.

 

Quarantine a Modem

You must have special permissions to use the Quarantine function.

To quarantine a modem:

Navigate to the applicable modem.

On the device Summary tab, click Quarantine in the Actions Menu. RIO displays the Quarantine Device dialog box.

Select a Quarantine Reason from the drop-down list.

Click Quarantine. RIO quarantines the modem and updates the status on the device Summary page.
